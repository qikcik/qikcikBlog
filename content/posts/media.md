# Media

(my short observations about the artifacts created by other people, please keep in mind that following text is full of spoilers in classical way of understanding this term.) 


## 24.03.2026: Forrest Gump (1994)

This is one of those movies that’s hard to put into words. And one of those I’ll definitely return to. The narrative execution is brilliant.
Narrative glue in the form of scenes on the bench, humorous consistent short snippets that almost break the 4th wall, the pacing, and the fact that nothing is shown by accident. it all just keeps you in constant tension. 
It’s like taking the best parts of watching TikToks, but without any of the flaws.

The film flawlessly exposes that pure rationalization and conventional logic—both intellectual and social—is just a narrowed perspective on a broader subject. 
Despite being perceived differently from the outside, the protagonist consistently follows a path of courage, loyalty, integrity, and honesty. He doesn't do this mindlessly. he does it intelligently and with a sense of hope that infects everyone around him.

What resonated with me deeply is how crucial an authority figure is, in the meaning of someone who shows you how to navigate the world and focus on what truly matters. (in the movie it was a mother of main character)
The movie effectively points out how important action and effort are. And how important it is to "be more" and authentically put your whole self into everything you do, even if others might see it as stupid.

For me, the film also perfectly demonstrates that great things are simple things, and sometimes simple solutions are the best.

## 22.03.2026: Arrival (2016)

The most interesting thing is how fast this movie flew by, despite the complete lack of action or emotional plot twists.
It's not a movie I will ever return to, but it has an interestingly calm narrative pacing, and a main thread that forces you to think in the background after watching it.
The film has a lot of logical inconsistencies like the lack of explanation on how a written language doesn't contain the concept of time, or expecting a translation of an unknown alien language based on a few seconds of audio at the very beginning.
(I consciously decided not to analyze every detail of the world-building. It seems that this is not the type of movie where the created world holds the actual value)

The strongest point for me was that somewhere halfway through, I actually got a bit lost in the story's continuity.
I started wondering if the "scenes" from the beginning were actually at the beginning, or if I dreamed them, or if my memory just got completely messed up.
This resonated heavily with the main thread: that language is the main factor in our perception of the whole world.

Paradoxically to all this, the movie left me with one final query: "if I knew exactly what my whole life would look like in detail, and that it would end tragically, would I still be able to enjoy the small moments?"
